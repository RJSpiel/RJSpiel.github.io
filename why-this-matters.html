Why This Matters

THE PROBLEM
NO ONE FIXED

Independent auto shops keep vehicles on the road. The real estate system is making it harder for them to exist. This is why ARES was built.

The Real Problem

BUILT BY SOMEONE
WHO SAW THE GAP

Independent auto shops don't fail because their owners lack skill. They stall because the real estate system was never built for them.

In most communities, an independent auto repair shop is one of the most practical businesses operating. It employs skilled tradespeople. It serves customers who can't afford dealer service rates. It keeps older vehicles running safely for another year, another season, another family. These are not marginal businesses. They are infrastructure.

And yet, when an independent shop owner tries to grow — to open a second location, to move out of a space they've outgrown, to find a lot where they can finally build something permanent — they run into a wall that was never supposed to be their problem.

"The biggest barrier to opening or expanding an automotive business isn't capital or demand — it's land. ARES is here to fix that."

— Raymond Spieldenner, Founder
🗺️

Zoning Maps That Don't Work for Automotive

Cities like Olympia have rezoned commercial corridors for mixed use — quietly eliminating automotive-appropriate land in high-traffic areas. Shop owners find out when it's too late to change course.

🧪

EPA Problems Discovered Mid-Deal

Most available land fails environmental screening because of prior industrial or automotive activity. The contamination is real. The discovery almost always comes after weeks of time and money have already been committed.

🔧

Tools Built for Everyone Else

CoStar. CREXi. LoopNet. These platforms weren't built for automotive compliance requirements. Using them for an automotive property search means manually chasing information that should be filtered before you ever see a listing.

💸

The Cost Is Real and Measurable

Failed due diligence. Wasted broker time. Deposits lost on sites that never worked. Independent shop owners absorb these costs silently, every cycle, because the right tool has never existed — until now.

The Core Belief

AN INFORMATION GAP,
NOT AN IMPOSSIBLE PROBLEM

None of these problems are unsolvable. They are information problems. The EPA history exists. The zoning classification is a matter of public record. Lot dimensions and configurations can be assessed before anyone visits a site.

The issue is that this information has never been organised specifically for automotive operators — and surfaced at the beginning of a search, not at the end of a deal.

When compliance screening happens first, independent shop owners stop losing deals they should never have been pursuing. They see fewer properties — and every one they see is one that can actually work.
