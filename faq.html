About ARES

WHAT ARES
IS AND DOES

What exactly does ARES do?

+

ARES is a hybrid property search platform and scouting service built specifically for independent automotive businesses. We screen commercial real estate listings for automotive zoning compliance, EPA soil history, traffic volume, and lot suitability — before properties reach you.

You can search vetted listings independently through the platform, or work directly with our team on a full scouting engagement where we manage the research and due diligence support from start to finish.

How is ARES different from just searching CoStar or CREXi myself?

+

General commercial real estate platforms like CoStar and CREXi are built for broad commercial activity — retail, office, industrial. They list properties and let you filter by price and size. They were not built to screen for automotive zoning classifications, EPA environmental history, or lot configurations suited to service bays and vehicle lifts.

When an independent shop owner uses these tools, they are applying general-purpose filters to a specific-compliance problem. The gap between those two things is where property deals go to die. ARES was built from the ground up around the compliance requirements of automotive operations — the filters that matter to a shop owner are built into the screening process, not added as an afterthought.

How do you know this is actually a problem independent shops face?

+

ARES was developed through direct customer discovery across more than 17 visits — with independent automotive businesses, auto mall dealerships, commercial real estate brokers, and city planners across the South Sound. The same compliance barriers came up consistently across every conversation: EPA disqualifications discovered mid-deal, land scarcity in viable locations, and commercial brokers unfamiliar with automotive-specific requirements.

The problem is not rare or regional — it is the standard experience for independent shops trying to grow. Those conversations are what shaped every part of how ARES works.

+

The first step is a no-cost discovery call where we understand your business type, target location, budget, and expansion requirements. From there, you can access vetted property listings through the platform independently, or engage our team for full scouting support.

+

ARES is not a brokerage and does not replace a commercial broker for the transaction itself. What ARES does is handle the compliance screening that most brokers are not equipped to do for automotive-specific requirements — so that when you do engage a broker, you are already working from a shortlist of viable properties rather than starting from scratch.

For clients who need broker support, ARES connects them with commercial specialists in our network who have direct experience with automotive real estate. You get a specialist, not a generalist who is learning your industry alongside you.

What if a property looks good but I want a second opinion before committing?

+

That is exactly what the due diligence support component of ARES is for. Once you have identified a property you want to pursue seriously, ARES connects you with environmental consultants, zoning attorneys, and commercial brokers who can validate the site and support a protected offer.

The goal is that you never reach the closing stages of a deal without having the right specialists involved at the right time — not scrambling to find them after a problem surfaces.

Technical Questions

EPA, ZONING,
AND COMPLIANCE

What does "EPA cleared" actually mean for a property?

+

Automotive operations — including fuel storage, vehicle fluids, and parts cleaning — are regulated land uses. Many sites that appear available have prior contamination from past industrial or automotive activity, which can trigger environmental remediation requirements or outright disqualify a site for automotive use.

ARES screens for EPA soil history and environmental designations upfront, so you are not discovering a contamination problem at the point of due diligence — after money and time have already been committed to the process.

Why has automotive-zoned land become harder to find?

+

Over the past decade, many cities — including Olympia — have rezoned commercial corridors from industrial or automotive-specific classifications to mixed-use designations. Land that used to support automotive operations is no longer zoned for it. This change is not always obvious from a property listing, and it is not something a generalist broker will necessarily check before recommending a site.

Additionally, grandfathered automotive properties can lose their special use status after a vacancy period as short as one year — meaning even existing shop buildings need to be checked carefully before a long-term commitment is made.
